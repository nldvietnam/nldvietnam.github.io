Technical Advisory Team meeting December 2016
Date: 20/12/2016
On 20th Dec 2016, National Legislative Development Project (NLD) and International Cooperation Department of Ministry of Justice (ICD, MOJ) organized a Technical Advisory Team meeting (TAT meeting). The meeting was co-chaired by Ms. Kimberly Inksater – NLD Project Director and Ms. Tran Thu Huong – Deputy Director General of ICD, MOJ. Representatives from partner agencies, including Ministry of Justice, Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development, Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Industry and Trade, Office of the Government and Office of National Assembly.

In the presentation on the project progress, Ms. Kimberly Inksater informed all participants that until 15th Dec 2016, in year 2 Phase 2 of the Project, despite some constraints in project procedures, 75% of all activities were completed or under completion.

Representatives of partners also informed the progress of activities in a positive way. Partners committed to quickly conduct unimplemented activities before the end of the fiscal year 2016-2017 of the project.

One of the most outstanding activities of the Project is the study mission to Canada with the participation of officials from Ministry of Finance and Ministry of Justice. Head of mission delegation – Mr. Dang Cong Khoi, Deputy Director General of Ministry of Finance had a presentation on the content of discussion and experience on budget allocation, policy impact assessment, cost-effectiveness analysis, monitoring law implementation.

Ms. Tran Thu Huong informed participants of the situation of legal cooperation, collecting proposals from partners, timeline to send official proposals to NLD and introduced criteria to select activities.

Mr. Jacob Gammelgaard – NLD Project Field Manager notified that NLD would revise the procedure for recruitment of consultants by applying the public tender process where qualified consultants will submit their applications as individual candidates. This process would help ensure the transparency of the selection of consultants and broaden the application coverage and improve the quality of consultants to be recruited for project activities.

To end the meeting, parties agreed to quickly implement remaining activities of Annual Workplan 2016-2017 and develop Annual Workplan 2017-2018.

CALL FOR PROPOSALS
Date:14/12/2016
Calls for companies to develop and support software to manage and monitor the promulgation of legal normative documents.

The National Legislative Development Project (NLD Project) was designed, in collaboration with the Vietnamese government, to address the issues of quality, consistency and transparency in the law-making process and to make legislation more enforceable and effective. In particular, the project examines four core components of the law-making process: (1) Planning; (2) Policy Development; (3) Legislative Drafting; and (4) Legal System Coherence.  The project is funded by the Canadian Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development (DFATD).

In order to support the Ministry of Justice to carry out the new task, which was assigned by the Decision 921/QD-TTg dated 12 June 2013 of the Prime Minister, NLD Project will support the development of software to manage and monitor the promulgation of LNDs. The software is a web application system, that enable legal drafters, planners and managers to write legal documents (collaborative writing), by consulting and referencing other legal documents (document management) according to the legal obligations (workflow) while being able to report their activity (project management) in a timely manner. The main content is about the implementation of a tracking tool for the process of drafting of legal normative document (LNDs). In the phase one it encompasses the tracking of law and ordinance implementing legal normative documents at the central level.  In the future (phase 2), this application will have to support the monitor of all legal normative documents at the central level and law and ordinance implementing legal normative documents at the local level.

In phase 1 of the project (support the tracking law and ordinance implementing legal normative documents at central level), for each type of legal normative documents of the system, we must be able to track which agency it belongs to, who is assigned to work on it, what step a document is now rendered at. The tool should enable drafting agencies to develop detailed plans and monitor the development of Legal Normative Documents (LNDs) against the plans to ensure timely issuance of sufficient LNDs to meet the requirements for the implementation of laws and ordinances and law and ordinance making agenda.

The NLD now invites interested companies to submit proposals to develop and support a web application system project to manage and monitor the promulgation of legal normative documents.

The companies interested in responding should request an information package by contacting hr@nldvietnam.org.

Applications for bidding should be in Vietnamese, submitted directly or by post in both paper and electronic format (saved on CD disk) to NLD office at the following address:

Mrs. Nguyen Vu Thao Nguyen, The National Legislative Development Project, Room 1704, 53 Quang Trung Building, Hai Ba Trung, Hanoi

Any other information, activities of NLD, can be found at nldvietnam.org. 

The deadline for submissions is date 12 January 2017 at 16.00. 

Meeting to introduce Gender Equality Working Group
Date: 06/10/2016
On 6th October 2016, Ministry of Justice and National Legislative Development Project (NLD) organized a meeting to introduce the Expert Group on evaluating gender mainstreaming in policy development and legislative drafting (Gender Equality Working Group – GEWG). GEWG is established by Decision 1985/QD-BTP by Minister of Justice dated 21st September 2016.

The meeting was chaired by Mr. Nguyen Hong Tuyen, Director General of Department of General Affairs of Legislative Development (GALD), Ministry of Justice and Mr. Jacob Gammelgaard, NLD Project Field Manager. At the meeting, Mr. Do Dinh Luong  from GALD annouced the Decision 1985/QD-BTP and introduced the group members. GEWG members come from Ministry of Justice, Ministry of Labours, Invalids and Social Affairs, Ministry of Home Affairs, Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development, Ministry of Industry and Trade and Department of Social Affairs of Office of National Assembly.

Main functions of the GEWG is to conduct gender impact assessment, gender analysis in accordance with Law on Promulgation of Legal Normative Documents 2015 (LPLND 2015) and Decree 34/2016/ND-CP dated 14th May 2016 of the Government detailing some articles and implementation measures of LPLND 2015; provide guidance and conduct trainings on gender mainstreaming in law making; review legislations and recommend solutions to improve the gender mainstreaming in law making; study, revise the toolkit of gender mainstreaming in law making.

On behalf of NLD, Mr. Gammelgaard emphasized that the establishment of the GEWG and the implementation of their activities would improve the capacity of NLD partners as well as other agencies which have leading roles in gender mainstreaming and reassured the support of NLD in both technical and financial terms for GEWG to complete their tasks.

It can be said that this is the first inter-ministerial working group about gender mainstreaming in law making in Vietnam, a breakthrough in gender mainstreaming in lawmaking, which contribute to the implementation of the gender equality of NLD Project.

Meeting between Vice Minister of Justice Dinh Trung Tung and National Legislative Development Project Office
Date: 17/08/2016
On 17th August 2016, Vice Minister of Justice Dinh Trung Tung met with Ms. Kimberly Inksater – Project Director of National Legislative Development Project (NLD Project), Mr. Isabeau Vilandre – Former NLD Project Field Manager, Mr. Jacob Gammelgaard - NLD Project Field Manager and Ms. Harriet Roos – First Secretary of Canadian Embassy in Vietnam.

Mr. Tung reiterated some achievements of NLD Project and Ministry of Justice in the past 3 years when Mr. Isabeau was the NLD Project Field Manager, one of which was NLD’s support for the development and adoption of Law on Promulgation of Legal Normative Documents 2015. Mr. Tung praised Mr. Vilandre’s contributions to the legislative development in Vietnam and wished Mr. Vilandre success and opportunities to utilize valued experience from Vietnam legislative development in his new job.

Vice Minister Dinh Trung Tung and Mr. Isabaeu Vilandre

Mr. Tung congratulated Mr. Gammelgaard on his new position as the NLD Project Field Manager and wished him to timely get used to work and continue the success of NLD Project.

In a response, Mr. Vilandre and Mr. Gammelgaard thanked Mr. Tung and emphasized that the work transition in NLD Office were being conducted quickly and effectively so that upcoming activities would be implemented in compliance with Annual Workplan 2016-2017 to implement the Law on Promulgation of Legal Normative Documents effectively.
